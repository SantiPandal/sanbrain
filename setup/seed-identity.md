# Seed Your Identity

The book-mirror and daily-compile skills need to know WHO YOU ARE to make personal connections. Without identity files, the right column of every mirror will be generic.

## Required Files

Create these in your vault root:

### soul.md

Write a document covering:
- Who you are (age, location, profession, wiring)
- How you think (mental models, thinkers you follow, reasoning style)
- What you're building (active businesses, projects, goals)
- Your edges and blind spots (be honest — this is what makes mirrors valuable)
- How you process information
- What success looks like (12 months, 5 years, 10 years)

Length: as long as needed. This is the most important file in your vault. The more specific and honest it is, the better every skill performs.

### personality.md (optional but powerful)

If you've done psychological assessments (Big Five, values, attachment style, etc.), put the results here. Skills use this for:
- Book mirrors that reference your actual psychological patterns
- Entity propagation that flags blind-spot-relevant interactions
- Daily compilation that surfaces patterns you might miss

## How Skills Use These Files

- `book-mirror` reads both files to write the right column
- `daily-compile` reads soul.md to surface connections through your frameworks
- `context-for-claude-code` references your goals to suggest daily focus
- `enrich` uses your business context to determine entity relevance

## Maintenance

Update soul.md when:
- A business changes state (new revenue, new partner, pivot, shutdown)
- Your goals change
- You discover a new blind spot
- A major life event changes your context

The compiled truth principle applies to soul.md too: rewrite the current state, don't just append.
