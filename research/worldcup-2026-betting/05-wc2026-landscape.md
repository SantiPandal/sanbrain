## 1. The Field: 48 Teams, 12 Groups

The first 48-team World Cup runs June 11 – July 19, 2026 across the USA (11 cities), Mexico (3) and Canada (2). 104 total matches: 72 group games, then a 32-team knockout (16 R32 + 8 R16 + 4 QF + 2 SF + third-place + final) ([FIFA](https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026), [Sky Sports](https://www.skysports.com/football/news/12010/13272067/2026-world-cup-dates-venues-host-cities-and-format-for-usa-canada-and-mexico-tournament)).

**Final groups** (draw Dec 5, 2025, completed by March 2026 playoffs; per [FIFA standings](https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/standings), [FOX Sports](https://www.foxsports.com/soccer/fifa-world-cup/standings), [NBC Sports](https://www.nbcsports.com/soccer/news/2026-world-cup-group-stage-table-full-standings-for-all-12-groups)):

- **B:** Canada, Switzerland, Qatar, Bosnia & Herzegovina
- **C:** Brazil, Morocco, Haiti, Scotland
- **D:** USA, Paraguay, Australia, Türkiye
- **E:** Germany, Curaçao, Côte d'Ivoire, Ecuador
- **F:** Netherlands, Japan, Tunisia, Sweden
- **G:** Belgium, Egypt, Iran, New Zealand
- **H:** Spain, Cabo Verde, Saudi Arabia, Uruguay
- **I:** France, Senegal, Norway, Iraq
- **J:** Argentina (holders), Algeria, Austria, Jordan
- **K:** Portugal, Uzbekistan, Colombia, DR Congo
- **L:** England, Croatia, Ghana, Panama

**March 2026 playoff outcomes** ([Wikipedia qualification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_FIFA_World_Cup_qualification), [NBC Sports](https://www.nbcsports.com/soccer/news/2026-world-cup-inter-confederation-playoffs-how-do-they-work-when-are-they-and-whos-involved)):
- **UEFA Path A:** Bosnia & Herzegovina beat Wales on pens, then **eliminated Italy 4-1 on penalties after 1-1** in the final. **Italy miss a third consecutive World Cup** — the single biggest qualification shock.
- **Path B:** Sweden beat Ukraine 3-1, then Poland 3-2 (no Lewandowski at this World Cup).
- **Path C:** Türkiye 1-0 Romania, then 1-0 Kosovo.
- **Path D:** Czechia beat Ireland and then Denmark, both on penalties.
- **Inter-confederation:** DR Congo beat Jamaica (first WC in 52 years); Iraq beat Bolivia (first since 1986).

Norway qualified directly with an 8/8 campaign (37 goals; Haaland 16 — equalling Lewandowski's single-campaign European qualifying record), sealing it with a 4-1 win in Italy in Nov 2025 — first Norwegian World Cup since 1998 ([beIN](https://www.beinsports.com/en-us/soccer/uefa-world-cup-qualifiers/articles/norway-and-haaland-end-a-28-year-wait-and-qualify-for-the-2026-world-cup-2025-11-16)). Debutants: Curaçao, Cabo Verde, Jordan, Uzbekistan.

## 3. Venues, Regions, Environment

Sixteen venues in three regional clusters ([FIFA stadium info](https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/articles/stadium-information-details), [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/sports/2026/6/3/fifa-world-cup-2026-stadiums-matches-us-canada-mexico)). FIFA strips sponsor names. Capacities as reported, vary slightly by source.

**West:** Vancouver — BC Place (~54,500, retractable roof, climate-flexible); Seattle — Lumen Field (~69,000, open-air); San Francisco Bay Area — Levi's Stadium (~70,900, open-air, temporary real grass); Los Angeles — SoFi (~70,000, fixed translucent roof, open sides, **no climate control**).

**Central:** Guadalajara — Estadio Akron (48,000, open-air, ~1,500 m altitude); Mexico City — Estadio Azteca/Banorte (87,523 post-renovation, open-air, **~2,200 m / 7,200 ft altitude** — the tournament's altitude extreme); Monterrey — Estadio BBVA (53,529, open-air bowl, no AC); Houston — NRG (~72,000, retractable roof + AC); Dallas — AT&T Stadium (92,967 — biggest venue, retractable roof + AC, most matches: 9 incl. a semifinal); Kansas City — Arrowhead (~76,400, open-air).

**East:** Toronto — BMO Field (45,736, smallest); Boston — Gillette (~65,900, open-air); New York/New Jersey — MetLife (82,500 per FIFA, open-air; hosts the final); Philadelphia — Lincoln Financial Field (~69,000, open-air); Atlanta — Mercedes-Benz (~75,000, retractable roof + AC); Miami — Hard Rock (~65,300, canopy over stands but **no AC**).

**Heat:** Climate analyses flag **Monterrey, Arlington (Dallas) and Houston** as the highest raw heat-stress sites, but Dallas/Houston/Atlanta/Vancouver are climate-controlled indoors — so the worst *on-pitch* risk is **Monterrey and Miami**, plus open-air US venues in heat waves. Miami's 5–6pm local kickoffs were called "a big mistake" by heat researchers. NPR/Climate Central count **67 of 104 matches at venues/times with heat-illness potential, 39 high-risk** ([Climate Central](https://www.climatecentral.org/climate-matters/world-cup-stadiums), [NPR June 4](https://www.npr.org/2026/06/04/nx-s1-5742519/world-cup-fifa-hot-weather-risk-climate-miami), [Bloomberg](https://www.bloomberg.com/graphics/2026-fifa-world-cup-games-weather/)).

## 6. 2026-Specific Competition Rules

- **Squads:** 26 players (min 3 GKs), locked June 1; **injury replacements allowed until 24h before a team's first match** (how Timber→Geertruida happened); GKs replaceable for serious injury throughout ([Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/sports/2026/5/8/world-cup-2026-squad-deadlines-key-dates-team-lists-final-announcements)).
- **Substitutions:** 5 in 90' (max 3 windows) + 1 extra in ET.
- **Advancement:** top two from each of 12 groups **+ 8 best third-placed teams** → Round of 32. ⚠️ **Tiebreaker order discrepancy across sources:** this chapter's sources ([FIFA](https://www.fifa.com/en/tournaments/mens/worldcup/canadamexicousa2026/articles/groups-how-teams-qualify-tie-breakers), [NBC Philadelphia](https://www.nbcphiladelphia.com/world-cup/2026-fifa-world-cup-tiebreaker-rules/4394025/)) list points → overall GD → overall goals → then head-to-head; Chapter 4's sources (NBC Sports/FOX) report a 2026 change to head-to-head-first. **Resolve against the official FIFA regulations PDF before modeling MD3 scenarios** — it changes qualification probabilities in tight groups.
- **Knockouts (R32 onward):** two 15-minute ET periods if level, then penalties ([NBC Sports](https://www.nbcsports.com/soccer/news/2026-world-cup-overtime-extra-time-penalty-kick-rules)).
- **Group finales kick off simultaneously** within each group (six games/day June 24–27).
- **New IFAB rules in force:** expanded VAR powers, 10-second substitution rule, 8-second GK hold = corner, captain-only referee dialogue, hydration breaks ([Sky](https://www.skysports.com/football/news/12098/13549645/world-cup-ifab-confirm-new-var-powers-10-second-substitutions-and-tactical-timeout-ban-in-major-rule-changes)).
