# Betting Market Mechanics and Quantitative Execution — A Trader's Manual

*Chapter 3 of the WC2026 quantitative betting dossier. Prices and landscape verified June 9, 2026, two days before Mexico–South Africa opens the FIFA World Cup at Estadio Azteca.*

The premise of this dossier is that a betting market is just a derivatives market with worse counterparties and better marketing. Every market in this chapter should be read the way a trader reads an order book: a price, an implied probability, a transaction cost, and a counterparty whose flow is either informed or recreational. Your entire job is to (1) convert prices into probabilities correctly, (2) find instruments where the marginal price-setter is recreational, and (3) size positions so variance cannot kill a true edge.

---

## 1. Odds, implied probability, and the overround

**Conversions.** Decimal odds *d* = total return per 1 staked. Implied probability = 1/*d*. American odds: for +A, *d* = 1 + A/100; for −A, *d* = 1 + 100/A. Fractional odds f = d − 1. So −110 → d = 1.909 → 52.38% implied; +333 → d = 4.33 → 23.09%; +450 → d = 5.50 → 18.18%.

**The overround (vig).** A bookmaker's quoted probabilities sum to more than 1. Overround = Σ(1/dᵢ) − 1. Worked example from the actual tournament opener, Mexico vs South Africa (June 11), at bet365 per [Squawka's June preview](https://www.squawka.com/us/news/world-cup/match-preview-mexico-vs-south-africa-06-11-26-world-cup-2026/): Mexico −209, Draw +333, South Africa +550 → 67.6% + 23.1% + 15.4% = **106.1%**, a 6.1% overround (~5.8% margin on handle). The same fixture on prediction markets trades near **100.5%** (Mexico ≈ 69.5¢, Draw ≈ 20.5¢, per [DefiRate's tracker](https://defirate.com/prediction-markets/world-cup-odds/mexico/)) — the "vig" there lives in the bid–ask spread and fees instead. Sharp books occupy the middle: [Pinnacle runs ~1.5–2.5% on major football 1X2](https://www.rantsports.com/ca/betting/sportsbook-review/pinnacle/), and US retail books run roughly 4.5–7% on soccer three-ways ([Pinnacle's margin calculator](https://www.pinnacle.com/betting-resources/en/betting-tools/margin-calculator) is the standard tool). Two-way markets: −105/−105 ≈ 2.4% overround (sharp), −110/−110 ≈ 4.54% (retail standard).

**Futures margins are enormous — verified live.** Summing implied probabilities on the DraftKings/FanDuel WC2026 outright (prices June 8–9 via [CBS Sports](https://www.cbssports.com/betting/news/world-cup-odds/), [SI](https://www.si.com/betting/every-teams-odds-to-win-the-2026-world-cup-spain-set-as-favorites-ahead-of-opening-match) and [Yahoo](https://sports.yahoo.com/soccer/betting/article/2026-world-cup-odds-for-all-48-teams-to-win-the-title-145427384.html)): Spain +450 (18.2%), France +475 (17.4%), England +700 (12.5%), Portugal +850 (10.5%), Brazil +900 (10.0%), Argentina +900 (10.0%), Germany +1400 (6.7%), Netherlands +2200 (4.3%), Norway +3500 (2.8%), Belgium +3500 (2.8%), Colombia +4000 (2.4%), Morocco +5000 (2.0%), Uruguay +5000 (2.0%), Mexico +5500 (1.8%), USA +6000 (1.6%), Japan +6500 (1.5%), Switzerland +6500 (1.5%), Croatia +8000 (1.2%), Ecuador +10000 (1.0%), Senegal +10000 (1.0%), Canada +22500 (0.4%). **Those 21 teams alone sum to ≈ 111.6%.** Add the remaining 27 qualifiers at +8000 to +100000 and the full 48-outcome book lands roughly in the **low-to-mid 120s percent — a 20–25%+ overround**, versus 6% on the match 1X2. Golden Boot and correct-score books are worse still (30–60% is normal). This is the single most important transaction-cost fact in tournament betting: *the margin is not spread evenly — it is concentrated in the longshots* (see Shin, below).

**Removing the vig.** Three standard methods:

- **Proportional/multiplicative:** pᵢ = (1/dᵢ)/Σ(1/dⱼ). Fast, fine for low-margin two-way markets (Asian handicaps, totals at Pinnacle) where margin is roughly symmetric.
- **Power method:** solve k such that Σ(1/dᵢ)^k = 1; pᵢ = (1/dᵢ)^k. Shrinks longshots more than favorites.
- **Shin's method:** models the book as facing a fraction *z* of insiders, derives prices that load margin onto longshots; invert iteratively to recover probabilities. It explicitly corrects the favorite-longshot bias.

When to use which: empirical comparisons (Joseph Buchdahl's "Wisdom of the Crowd" framework, implemented in the [R `implied` package](https://cran.r-project.org/web/packages/implied/vignettes/introduction.html)) find [Shin works best for sharp/low-margin books like Pinnacle and bet365, power for several soft books, and that above decimal odds ≈ 5.3 Shin/power beat naive normalization by ~1.5% in predictive accuracy](https://medium.com/geekculture/how-to-compute-football-implied-probabilities-from-bookmakers-odds-bbb33ccf7c1d). Rule of thumb: multiplicative for balanced two-ways; Shin or power any time the market includes longshots — 1X2 with a big favorite, correct score, and especially outrights. De-vigging a WC outright multiplicatively will systematically overstate longshots' fair probability and make terrible bets look +EV.

## 2. Market taxonomy for a World Cup — sharpness, margin, limits

| Market | Description | Sharpness/liquidity | Typical margin | Typical limits |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Asian handicap** (incl. ¼ lines: −0.25 = half stake −0 / half −0.5) | Two-way, draw removed or split | **Sharpest market in football**; where Asian books and syndicates trade | 1.5–2.5% sharp; ~4.5% retail | Highest: five to six figures at sharp books for WC matches |
| **Totals (O/U 2.5 etc.)** | Two-way goals line | Nearly as sharp as AH | 2–5% | High, similar to AH |
| **1X2 (three-way)** | Win/draw/win | Sharp at close for big matches | 2% (Pinnacle) – 7% (retail; 6.1% verified on the opener) | High: $5k–$25k+ retail on marquee WC games |
| **Draw no bet / Double chance** | Derivatives of 1X2 | Priced off 1X2; rarely independently sharp | Similar to 1X2, often slightly worse | Medium-high |
| **BTTS** | Both teams to score | Semi-sharp | 5–8% | Medium |
| **Correct score / HT-FT** | Exact outcomes | Soft; recreational | 10–30%+ | Low ($500–$2k) |
| **Player props** (shots, SOT, goalscorer, cards) | Player-level lines | **Soft**; priced by models + trader judgment, thin | 7–20%; anytime-scorer books frequently 25–40% summed | Low ($250–$1k typical) |
| **Team props** (corners, cards) | Team-level counts | Soft-to-medium | 6–12% | Low-medium |
| **Futures** (outright, group winner, to-reach-stage, Golden Boot, Golden Ball) | Tournament derivatives | Liquid in headline outright only; derivative books stale | **20–60%+** (≈ 20–25% verified on the outright above; specials worse) | Low max-win on longshots |
| **Same-game parlays** | Correlated intra-match combos | **Softest product in the building**; correlations priced with a house buffer | Effective hold 15–20%+ per operator reports vs ~5% straights | Capped payouts |

**The sharpness hierarchy** is the map of where information lives: *closing Asian handicap and totals at Pinnacle/Asian books > closing 1X2 > opening lines > BTTS/team props > player props > correct score/HT-FT > futures derivatives > SGPs.* The sharper the market, the more your counterparty is Starlizard; the softer, the more it's a guy in a Pulisic jersey. Trade accordingly: take your *opinions* to soft markets and your *price benchmark* from sharp ones.

## 3. Market efficiency: what the evidence actually says

**Closing lines are close to efficient.** The canonical study is [Kaunitz, Zhong & Kreiner (2017), "Beating the bookies with their own numbers"](https://arxiv.org/abs/1710.02824): average closing odds across bookmakers were so well calibrated that simply betting outlier prices against the consensus returned **+3.5% over 56,435 historical bets and +8.5% over 265 real-money bets** — after which [bookmakers limited their accounts to stakes as small as ~$1 within months](https://www.digit.fyi/boffins-bookies-banhammer/). Two lessons in one paper: (a) the closing consensus is the best public estimate of fair value; (b) retail books defend their margins with discrimination, not better pricing.

**Closing line value (CLV) is the edge proxy.** Because the close aggregates all information and sharp flow, consistently beating the closing price (after vig) at a sharp book is the strongest single predictor of long-run profit — far better than short-run P&L, which is mostly noise at realistic sample sizes ([Pinnacle on tracking](https://www.pinnacle.com/betting-resources/en/educational/how-to-track-your-sports-betting-results-to-find-an-edge); [Trademate on the closing line](https://tradematesports.medium.com/closing-line-the-most-important-metric-in-sports-trading-58e56cdb4458)). Practitioner heuristic: [bettors who beat Pinnacle's close by ~2%+ on average are profitable long-term](https://betherosports.com/blog/how-to-use-pinnacle).

**The favorite-longshot bias (FLB).** Documented in football since [Cain, Law & Peel (2000)](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=234996): favorites win more often than implied prices suggest, longshots less often, in both match results and correct scores. Magnitudes across studies: backing strong favorites loses roughly the margin (low single digits per bet), while backing big-priced longshots loses 20–40%+ of stakes — and the bias is most extreme exactly where WC bettors love to play (correct scores, +10000 outrights, anytime scorers). Notably, [evidence from Asian handicap betting (Whelan)](https://www.karlwhelan.com/Papers/RBF.pdf) shows returns much flatter across price ranges in AH — the bias largely disappears in the market where sharps set prices. Implication: the *structure* of mispricing is predictable — fade longshots, or at minimum never buy them at retail futures prices.

**Where inefficiency persists:** (1) **opening/early lines**, before sharp volume arrives — this is why limits open low; (2) **props and niche markets**, where models are crude and limits cap the incentive to sharpen them; (3) **live/in-play**, where algorithmic repricing overreacts to red cards/early goals and latency creates stale quotes (see [in-game bettor behavior evidence](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2202.10085)); (4) **soft retail books** slow to follow steam; (5) **promos/boosts**, which are deliberately −EV for the house as marketing — the only retail product where +EV is handed to you.

## 4. Sharp practice: execution

**Line shopping is the cheapest edge in the business.** Moving from −110 to −105 cuts the break-even win rate from 52.38% to 51.22%; across the industry the consensus estimate is that shopping 5–10 books adds [0.5–2% to ROI — which can be 10–50% of a professional's entire margin](https://www.bettingusa.com/sports/line-shopping/). During a World Cup, price dispersion across 8+ US books on props and derivatives is routinely 5–20 cents; an odds screen is mandatory infrastructure, not a convenience.

**Exchanges.** [Betfair](https://www.betfair.com/exchange/plus/en/football/fifa-world-cup-betting-12469077) is the global benchmark: peer-to-peer back/lay with [commission on net market winnings at a 5% base rate in UK/Ireland](https://support.betfair.com/app/answers/detail/413-exchange-what-is-commission-and-how-is-it-calculated/) (discounts apply), no one banned for winning, and World Cup match-odds markets that [commonly match tens of millions of pounds per big game](https://www.betfairtradingblog.com/world-cup-football-tournament/) — aggregate tournament turnover runs to the hundreds of millions. Not available to US residents.

**The US "exchange" landscape, June 2026 — verified:**
- **Kalshi**: CFTC-regulated event-contract exchange, live nationally, [offering WC2026 markets across the US, Canada and Mexico](https://www.cbssports.com/prediction/news/kalshi-world-cup-2026/) — tournament winner, match markets, advancement. On April 6, 2026 the Third Circuit [affirmed that its sports event contracts are CEA "swaps" under exclusive CFTC jurisdiction, preempting New Jersey](https://www.hklaw.com/en/insights/publications/2026/04/federal-appeals-court-cftc-jurisdiction-over-sports-event-contracts), though Massachusetts has banned its sports contracts and Arizona has filed criminal charges — the state-vs-federal fight is live, and [Congress is pressuring the CFTC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/04/30/congress-kalshi-polymarket-prediction-markets-cftc.html).
- **Polymarket**: back in the US as a regulated venue after its [$112M acquisition of CFTC-licensed QCEX](https://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/polymarket-acquires-cftc-licensed-exchange-and-clearinghouse-qcex-for-112-million-302509626.html), [onboarding KYC'd US users since December 2025](https://www.ccn.com/news/crypto/polymarket-returns-to-us-here-is-whats-new/). Its WC winner market alone has matched [**$1.6 billion** as of June 5, 2026](https://www.kucoin.com/news/flash/as-of-june-5-2026-polymarket-s-world-cup-winner-contract-hits-1-6-billion-in-trading-volume), and US prediction markets are projected to do [$2.37B+ on this World Cup](https://www.rotowire.com/article/world-cup-prediction-markets-how-to-trade-on-the-biggest-sporting-event-117042). For outrights, ~100%-book prediction-market prices are dramatically better than a 120%+ sportsbook futures book.
- **Sporttrade**: [shut down its NJ/CO/IA/AZ/VA sportsbook operations on May 25, 2026 and is awaiting CFTC designation to relaunch nationally](https://www.gambling.com/us/news/sporttrade-exits-us-sports-betting-market-in-pivot-to-prediction-markets) — **not live for this World Cup**.
- **ProphetX**: peer-to-peer exchange operating under a [sweepstakes model in 40+ states (not AZ, ID, LA, MI, MT, NJ, NV, OH, TN, WA)](https://oddsassist.com/prediction-markets/prophetx-states/), itself [seeking CFTC status](https://sbcamericas.com/2025/11/10/prophetx-sports-prediction-market/). Useful prices, real regulatory risk.
- **Incumbent convergence**: [FanDuel Predicts launched with CME Group in December 2025 in five non-sportsbook states](https://flutter.com/news-media/press-releases/fanduel-and-fanduel-predicts/) ([press release](https://www.cmegroup.com/media-room/press-releases/2025/12/22/fanduel-and-cme-group-launch-fanduel-predicts.html)) and [DraftKings Predictions runs in 38 states through the same CME pipes](https://defirate.com/news/draftkings-launches-prediction-markets-apps/).

**Arbitrage and middling.** Cross-book arbs (back A at book 1, A-complement at book 2 with summed implied < 100%) appear constantly during high-volatility WC news cycles but burn account longevity. Middles — e.g., Over 2.25 at one book, Under 2.75 at another — risk a small margin to win both when exactly the middle number lands; quarter-line Asian handicaps make football unusually middle-rich.

**Steam and the food chain.** A "steam move" is synchronized sharp money hitting the market-making books (Pinnacle, Asian books, Circa), which reprice within seconds; slower retail books lag minutes. Steam-chasing is a real but fragile strategy — books fingerprint it. The originators are syndicates: Tony Bloom's **Starlizard** (UK High Court filings allege [~£600M/year in winnings, ~£1M staked per game, clients targeting 1–3% ROI on Asian-handicap volume](https://www.racingpost.com/news/britain/high-court-case-alleges-tony-blooms-betting-empire-makes-600m-a-year-so-what-do-we-know-about-his-starlizard-syndicate-aNlkE7t8daxQ/)) and Matthew Benham's **Smartodds** — both of whom turned betting models into Premier League clubs (Brighton, Brentford). Note their margins: the best operation on earth grinds 1–3%, on enormous volume, in the sharpest market. Calibrate your self-assessment to that.

**Why retail limits you.** US retail books are marketing companies running ~5–10% blended hold for recreational flow; a winner is an adverse-selection leak, so they cut stakes ("factoring") often within days to weeks of detectable CLV — the [Kaunitz experience](https://arxiv.org/abs/1710.02824) is the documented version. **Pinnacle's "winners welcome" model is the opposite**: low margin, high limits, no bans — it uses sharp flow as free price discovery ([profile](https://www.sharkbetting.com/blog/sharp-books-explained)). In the US, **Circa Sports** ([NV, CO, IL, IA, KY, MO](https://www.circasports.com/)) is the closest analog: [3–4% holds, $20k+ limits, actively courting sharp action](https://jedibets.com/sportsbooks/circa-odds). Exchanges and prediction markets structurally cannot ban winners. Plan your account topology around this: soft books are for harvesting, sharp venues are for benchmarking and size.

## 5. Bankroll and sizing: EV, Kelly, variance

**Expected value.** EV per unit staked = p·d − 1, where p is *your* probability and d the decimal price. Your edge only exists relative to the de-vigged market: edge = p_yours − p_fair(market). If you can't beat the de-vigged closing price, you have no edge by definition.

**Kelly criterion (sketch of derivation).** Stake fraction f of bankroll at net odds b = d − 1, win prob p, q = 1 − p. Expected log-growth: G(f) = p·ln(1 + fb) + q·ln(1 − f). Set G′(f) = pb/(1+fb) − q/(1−f) = 0 → **f\* = (pb − q)/b = edge/(d − 1)**. Example: your model says Spain wins the World Cup 22% of the time; DraftKings offers +450 (d = 5.5). EV = 0.22 × 5.5 − 1 = +21%. f\* = (0.22×4.5 − 0.78)/4.5 ≈ **4.7%** of bankroll; quarter-Kelly ≈ 1.2%.

**Why fractional Kelly (¼–½).** (1) Your p is estimated with error, and the growth curve is brutally asymmetric: betting 2× Kelly produces *zero* long-run growth, beyond that negative. (2) Drawdown control: at full Kelly the probability of ever falling to fraction a of your bankroll is ≈ a (a coin-flip chance of halving); at half Kelly ≈ a³ (12.5% chance of halving); at quarter Kelly ≈ a⁷ (<1%). Half Kelly sacrifices ~25% of growth for ~75% of the variance reduction — the standard professional compromise.

**Simultaneous and correlated bets — the World Cup trap.** A WC portfolio of Spain outright + Spain to win Group + Spain match moneylines is *one thesis stacked three times*: the bets are strongly positively correlated, so summed standalone Kelly stakes massively over-bet the true joint Kelly. Correct approach: maximize E[ln W] over the *joint* outcome distribution (enumerate scenarios: Spain wins group and tournament, group only, neither…), which always yields total exposure well below the sum of standalone stakes — as a crude rule, cap the whole correlated cluster near the standalone Kelly of your single strongest bet. Mutually exclusive bets (two different teams in the same outright) also interact: Kelly for an exclusive set is solved jointly, not bet-by-bet.

**Variance at realistic edges — the number every bettor underestimates.** Take a genuine 2% edge at −110 (true win rate 53.43%), 500 flat 1-unit bets: mean profit = +10u; per-bet SD ≈ 0.95u → portfolio SD ≈ 21.3u. **P(net loss after 500 bets) ≈ 32%**, and the 5th percentile is ≈ −25 units. You need roughly **6,000 bets** for 95% confidence that a true 2% edge shows any profit at all. This is why CLV, not P&L, is the performance metric, and why 30–50 unit drawdowns are routine, not evidence of a broken model.

**Break-even win rate by price** (= 1/d): −200 → 66.7% | −150 → 60.0% | −110 → 52.4% | −105 → 51.2% | +100 → 50.0% | +150 → 40.0% | +200 → 33.3% | +400 → 20.0% | +900 → 10.0% | +5000 → 1.96%.

**Record-keeping.** Log every bet: timestamp, book, market, price taken, stake, de-vigged closing price at Pinnacle/Betfair (or Kalshi/Polymarket mid for futures), result. Report (a) ROI, (b) average CLV in %, (c) CLV hit-rate. A bettor with +2% average CLV and negative P&L is healthy; the reverse is lucky ([CLV guide](https://www.sharpfootballanalysis.com/sportsbook/clv-betting/)).

## 7. The Jane Street framing, made explicit

1. **Everything is a probability.** Never think in odds formats; convert every quote to de-vigged implied probability (Shin/power for longshot-bearing markets) before any decision.
2. **Edge is a spread, not an opinion.** Your edge = your probability − market fair probability. No spread after vig, no trade. The opener: bet365's Mexico −209 is 67.6% raw, ≈ 64% de-vigged; you bet only if your model materially disagrees with 64%, not because "Mexico at the Azteca."
3. **Fair value is the sharp close.** Pinnacle/Betfair closing prices (Kalshi/Polymarket mids for futures) are your mark-to-market. Beating them — CLV — is your P&L *process* metric; realized P&L over a 104-match tournament is noise (a true 2% edge loses over 500 bets a third of the time).
4. **Size by edge over variance.** Kelly gives the growth-optimal fraction; estimation error and drawdown math say bet ¼–½ of it, and treat correlated tournament positions (outright + group + matches, same team) as one position.
5. **Trade where the counterparty is recreational.** The structural opportunity set for WC2026, in order: promos/boosts → player props and niche derivatives at soft books → early lines and live overreactions → prediction-market outrights vs 120%+ sportsbook futures books. Never pay a 25% overround for a longshot future a prediction market sells near fair.
6. **Protect your access.** Accounts at soft books are depleting assets; sharp venues (Circa, exchanges, prediction markets) are your durable infrastructure.

## Key takeaways for the betting playbook

- **Convert every price to implied probability and strip the vig before judging anything**; use multiplicative de-vigging only for tight two-ways, and Shin/power wherever longshots exist (1X2 favorites, correct score, all futures).
- **Margins vary by ~10× across markets**: ~2% on sharp Asian handicaps/totals, ~4.5–7% retail 1X2 (6.1% verified on bet365's Mexico–South Africa opener), 15–20%+ effective on SGPs, and **20–25%+ on the WC2026 outright** (21 verified DraftKings prices already sum to 111.6%).
- **The sharpness hierarchy is the map**: closing AH/totals at Pinnacle ≈ efficient; props, correct scores, HT/FT, futures and SGPs are where recreational flow sets prices — take opinions there, take benchmarks from the sharp close.
- **CLV is the metric, not P&L**: a 2% edge at −110 has a ~32% chance of losing money over 500 bets and needs ~6,000 bets for 95% significance; beating the de-vigged sharp close by ~2% is the professional bar.
- **The favorite-longshot bias is the most robust inefficiency in football** (Cain–Law–Peel and successors): longshots are systematically overpriced — losses of 20–40% of stakes at the extreme — and it is amplified by fat futures margins; structurally avoid buying retail longshots.
- **Line shopping adds 0.5–2% ROI for free**; −105 instead of −110 cuts the break-even rate by 1.16 points. Five-plus accounts and an odds screen are table stakes.
- **Promos are the most reliable +EV at this World Cup**: bet365's 2-Goals-Ahead early payout, FanDuel's $350 WC ladder, DK's bet-$5-get-$200, Fanatics' bet matches, Kalshi's bonus pools — convert bonuses at 60–75% cash equivalence and treat boosts as repriced options against the sharp line.
