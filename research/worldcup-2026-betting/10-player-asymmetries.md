## 5. Suspension and disciplinary risk map

**The 2026 rule change matters more than any individual player's temper.** FIFA confirmed in April a **double amnesty: single yellow cards are wiped after the group stage AND again after the quarterfinals** ([Al Jazeera Apr 29](https://www.aljazeera.com/sports/2026/4/29/fifa-confirms-new-world-cup-rule-on-yellow-cards-to-reduce-suspensions), [NBC Sports](https://www.nbcsports.com/soccer/news/2026-world-cup-yellow-card-rules-clean-slate-deadline)). Two yellows in separate matches (within a window) = one-match ban; the wipe applies to *single* cautions only. The exposure windows are therefore:

1. **Yellows on MD1+MD2 → banned for MD3** (the classic trap, unchanged);
2. **MD2+MD3 → banned for the R32** (accumulated suspensions carry; only single cards are wiped);
3. **R32+R16 → banned for the QF** — the new danger zone;
4. **QF single yellows are wiped → semifinals start clean** (so "suspended for the final" risk is nearly gone — a repricing the market may not have fully absorbed in "to reach final" player props).

Versus 2022 (single wipe after QF), this *halves* the historical base rate of stars missing knockout games — when modeling "player to be suspended" or team strength in R32/R16 sims, cut legacy suspension priors roughly in half.

**Who carries the risk** (defensive mids on contenders, tactical-foul roles): **Casemiro (BRA)** — 10 Premier League yellows in 2025-26 ([StatMuse](https://www.statmuse.com/fc/ask/casemiro-yellow-cards)); **Tchouaméni (FRA)** — repeat accumulation suspensions at Real Madrid across 2025 ([Tribuna](https://tribuna.com/en/news/2025-04-06-six-real-madrid-players-at-risk-of-suspension-ahead-of-arsenal-clash/), [Goal](https://www.goal.com/en-us/lists/real-madrid-defensive-crisis-mounts-aurelien-tchouameni-suspension-carlo-ancelotti-four-backline-options-mallorca/blt796decfeb42a1830)); **Rodri (ESP)** — the archetypal attack-interrupting six whose role generates bookings ([worldcuplocaltime](https://worldcuplocaltime.com/2026-world-cup-yellow-red-card-rules/)). Training-data priors (confirm before betting): Otamendi/Paredes (ARG), and Netherlands' makeshift back line producing more last-man fouls. "To be carded" props on these names in high-leverage MD3 spots where they sit one yellow from a ban are underpriced — the counter-case is managers pre-emptively benching.

**Referee/card-prop angle:** 52 referees — UEFA 15, CONMEBOL 12, with Argentina's Darío Herrera and Brazil's Raphael Claus the headline appointments ([worldcuplocaltime](https://worldcuplocaltime.com/2026-world-cup-referees/), [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_FIFA_World_Cup_officials)). Per-ref card baselines for this cycle publish only from June 11 — but the **Battle of Lusail (record 18 yellows, Mateu Lahoz, ARG–NED 2022)** lesson stands: card totals are a *referee* variable as much as a fixture variable; Lahoz averaged 4.62 yellows/game in his career against a WC match average around 3.5 ([ESPN](https://www.espn.com/soccer/story/_/id/37634744/netherlands-vs-argentina-world-cup-quarterfinal-sees-record-18-yellow-cards), [TBS](https://www.tbsnews.net/sports/mateu-lahoz-referee-who-has-shown-1542-yellow-cards-930781)). Process rule: when FIFA names officials (~48h out), look up the ref's domestic cards/game before touching any card prop — books set generic lines and adjust late. Assignment churn is live: a Somali referee was denied US entry June 8 ([Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/6/8/us-confirms-denying-entry-to-somali-referee-set-to-take-part-in-world-cup)).
