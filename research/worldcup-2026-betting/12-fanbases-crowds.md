# Fan Bases, Crowd Composition, and the Referee Channel — Who Actually Fills Each Stadium

**The thesis.** Chapter 2 established that a meaningful share of home advantage is crowd-mediated referee bias — penalties, 50/50 cards, added time — and that it scales with crowd size and hostility. This chapter operationalizes that: at a World Cup played across 16 North American venues, *nominal neutrality is a fiction*. Every fixture has a de facto home team determined by diaspora geography, ticket-sales economics, visa policy, and (in June 2026, uniquely) US immigration enforcement politics. Because FIFA gives each federation only **8% of stadium capacity** ([ESPN](https://www.espn.com/soccer/story/_/id/49008116/world-cup-tickets-iran-fans-revoked-says-country-federation), [FIFA ticketing](https://inside.fifa.com/news/world-cup-2026-new-ticket-pricing-tier-fans-qualified-teams)), the other ~90% of every crowd is set by *who already lives near the stadium and who can clear the US border*. Both are forecastable today, fixture by fixture — which makes crowd composition a real, mispriced performance variable.

## 1. The referee channel, quantified

The evidence base (recapping ch. 2 §1 and extending it):

- **Penalties:** 63% of 1,666 Premier League penalties over two decades went to the home side; behind closed doors in 2020–21 the bias measurably shrank and away wins (40%) exceeded home wins (38%) for the first time ever ([Nature Scientific Reports](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-00784-8)).
- **Crowd noise is the mechanism, experimentally:** 40 qualified referees judging identical challenges awarded **~15.5% fewer fouls against the home team when crowd noise was audible** — the noise didn't punish away players more, it *excused home players* ([Nevill, Balmer & Williams 2002](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/32117163_Influence_of_crowd_noise_on_soccer_refereeing_consistency_in_soccer)). [Unkelbach & Memmert (JSEP 2010)](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20733209/) replicated the effect on yellow-card decisions and tied it to real-game outcomes via database analysis.
- **Added time bends toward the crowd:** Spanish referees added roughly **twice as much injury time when the home team was a goal behind as when it was a goal ahead**, with the bias scaling with crowd size and attendance composition ([Garicano, Palacios-Huerta & Prendergast, REStat 2005](https://direct.mit.edu/rest/article-abstract/87/2/208/57539/Favoritism-Under-Social-Pressure)). In the post-2022 ~100-minute regime (ch. 2 §4), referee discretion over added time is *larger* than in that study's era, not smaller.
- **International evidence:** across World Cup qualification, home teams score **+0.44 goals/game**, and in African qualifiers referees issued significantly more red cards and penalties against away teams ([Int. J. of Performance Analysis in Sport, 2017](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/24748668.2017.1304031)).
- **World Cup hosts specifically:** the anecdotal record is notorious — Italy 1934, England 1966, Argentina 1978, and South Korea 2002 (Blatter himself called some of the officiating "a disaster") all featured host-friendly officiating controversies ([World Soccer](https://www.worldsoccer.com/world-cup/world-cup-controversies-402570), [FootballTransfers](https://www.footballtransfers.com/en/transfer-news/world-fifa-club-world-cup/2022/11/world-cup-refereeing-worst-controversial-calls-decisions)). ⚠️ *Honesty flag: no clean public dataset of "penalties awarded to hosts 1990–2022" could be verified; Qatar 2022 (hosts got nothing and lost all three) shows the channel needs a real partisan crowd plus a competitive team. Treat the sizing below as club-evidence extrapolation, not WC-measured.*

**Sizing the channel for a lopsided crowd (author's estimate).** At the VAR-era WC penalty rate of ~0.33–0.45/match (ch. 2 §4), shifting the award split from 50/50 toward the club-football home share (~60/40) is worth roughly **+0.04–0.08 expected penalties ≈ +0.03–0.06 goals** to the crowd-backed team; the card literature implies **~0.3–0.5 fewer yellows for the crowd team / more for its opponent**; added-time discretion adds a state-dependent kicker when the crowd team chases. Total: on the order of **+0.05–0.10 goals, i.e. ~1–3pp of win probability in an even match**. That is too small to bet 1X2 on its own — but it is *free directional edge* on penalty/card/added-time props, it compounds with altitude/heat/travel stacks (ch. 2 §2), and crucially **the market prices it for the three hosts but not for diaspora-backed "away" teams**. VAR does not neutralize it: crowd roars influence what gets reviewed and how marginal on-field calls are made in the first place, and 2026's expanded VAR powers ([Sky](https://www.skysports.com/football/news/12098/13549645/world-cup-ifab-confirm-new-var-powers-10-second-substitutions-and-tactical-timeout-ban-in-major-rule-changes)) widen the discretionary surface.

## 2. Who actually bought the tickets

**FIFA's own sales data** (phase press releases): by December 2025, **~2 million tickets** were sold across the Visa Presale and Early Ticket Draw, with buyers led by **USA, Canada, Mexico — then England, Germany, Brazil, Colombia, Spain, Argentina, France** ([FIFA, Dec 2025](https://inside.fifa.com/news/nearly-two-million-tickets-sold-world-cup-2026-third-phase-december)). The Random Selection Draw phase (Dec 11–Jan 13) drew a claimed **508 million ticket requests from 212 countries/territories**, with the same top-ten buyer list ([FIFA](https://inside.fifa.com/organisation/media-releases/over-500-million-ticket-requests-world-cup-2026-random-selection-draw), [OneFootball](https://onefootball.com/en/news/world-cup-2026-the-top-10-countries-that-bought-the-most-tickets-42012770), [Yahoo](https://sports.yahoo.com/soccer/article/fifa-claims-to-have-508-million-requests-for-7-million-available-tickets-so-why-are-plenty-of-tickets-still-available-235241494.html)). By May 2026, **5+ million of ~7 million tickets** were gone and the first-come-first-served Last-Minute Sales Phase was live ([EU Reporter](https://www.eureporter.co/lifestyle/sport/fifa/2026/05/05/fifa-faces-mounting-challenges-ahead-of-2026-world-cup-ticket-rush/), [Goal](https://www.goal.com/en/news/how-to-buy-world-cup-tickets/blt13fd3078b4ca56ce)).

Read-through for crowd prediction: **Colombia is a top-7 ticket-buying nation despite a 696-day US tourist-visa wait** (see §5) — that demand is overwhelmingly US-resident Colombians, i.e. it shows up at Hard Rock Stadium, not at the embassy in Bogotá. England and Germany rank high *and* are visa-exempt (ESTA) — their fans actually arrive. Mexico's demand is both domestic (Azteca/Guadalajara) and US-diaspora.

**Resale market signals (June 2026)** — the cleanest live measure of which fixtures are "owned":

- **Hottest:** the final (get-in **$7,291+** resale; FIFA's own dynamically-priced top seats hit ~$11k, having tripled to **$32,970** for top category in May) and — remarkably — **Colombia vs Portugal, Miami, June 27: get-in above $2,700, second only to the final** ([Goal most-expensive ranking](https://www.goal.com/en/news/most-expensive-world-cup-tickets/blt40665381e2b4c304), [worldcupwiki tickets](https://worldcupwiki.com/tickets/)). Argentina group tickets ~$2,475–2,925. All three Mexico group games plus Brazil–Morocco (MetLife) and Scotland–Brazil (Miami) showed **zero primary inventory by mid-May**.
- **Coldest:** more than half of group matches dropped **20%+ on resale** in the run-up, get-ins down to ~$157 ([Goal cheapest-tickets](https://www.goal.com/en-us/news/cheapest-world-cup-tickets/bltaf804f79f791b953)); **USA vs Paraguay (the US opener at SoFi, June 12) was still not sold out on June 8**, with 3,000+ resale listings — dynamic pricing set fan-hostile levels and demand didn't meet them ([NPR, June 8](https://www.npr.org/2026/06/08/nx-s1-5849905/fifa-world-cup-tickets-prices), [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/sports/2026/5/3/usa-vs-paraguay-among-world-cup-games-with-unsold-exorbitant-tickets), [The Hill](https://thehill.com/policy/keeping-score/5825926-expensive-world-cup-tickets/)).
- **Corporate layer:** official hospitality (On Location) starts ~$2,000/person/match — 110% above Qatar 2022 — runs to **$73,200/person** for the 8-match MetLife series including the final, and absorbs **up to ~35% of knockout-stage seats** ([fwctimes](https://fwctimes.com/world-cup-hospitality-packages/), [worldcupwiki hospitality](https://worldcupwiki.com/fifa-world-cup-2026-hospitality/)). The final at MetLife will be the most corporate, least partisan crowd of the tournament; group games in immigrant-belt cities will be the most partisan.

## 4. The hosts' crowds

**Mexico — the only true fortress.** Azteca (87,523 post-reno) is the loudest, most documented cauldron in CONCACAF — a three-tier bowl whose roof traps noise, at 2,240m, where Mexico is unbeaten in WC matches across 1970/1986 (ch. 2; [Estadio Azteca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Estadio_Azteca)). All three group games are at altitude in 90–95% partisan stadiums; all three showed zero primary inventory by mid-May. The kicker the market underweights: **Mexico is also the home team in any US stadium.** The benchmark is the 2011 Gold Cup *final* — USA vs Mexico at the Rose Bowl, **93,420, overwhelmingly pro-Mexico** ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2011_CONCACAF_Gold_Cup_Final), [Bleacher Report](https://bleacherreport.com/articles/748503-gold-cup-2011-mexico-repeats-as-winners-with-4-2-win-over-the-united-states)) — and El Tri has run a profitable US "home" friendly circuit for decades on exactly this demand. If Mexico wins Group A it stays at **Azteca for the R32 (June 30)**; as runner-up it gets **SoFi June 28** — in the county with America's largest Mexican-origin population, that is a 85–90% El Tri crowd against (plausibly) Switzerland. Either branch keeps the referee channel fully loaded through two knockout rounds; expected pro-Mexico shares: **~95% Azteca, 80–90% LA/Houston/Dallas, 70%+ anywhere else in the US** (author's estimate from the friendly/Gold Cup record and §3 census anchors).

**USA — softer than the narrative.** The opener vs Paraguay at SoFi was **still unsold on June 8** at $560+ face entry levels ([NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/06/08/nx-s1-5849905/fifa-world-cup-tickets-prices)); Clint Dempsey's warning — *"we were outnumbered in games that mattered"* — reflects Copa América 2024, where USA drew 47–59k while Argentina, Colombia and Brazil sold out NFL buildings ([Goal](https://www.goal.com/en/lists/clint-dempsey-usmnt-national-team-2026-world-cup/blt7763aacd5dbee09f), [attendance list](https://www.soccer-blogger.com/2024/07/08/copa-america-2024-crowds-list-of-attendances-for-all-ca-2024-soccer-matches-in-usa/)); Tyler Adams now spins hostile home crowds as useful preparation ([FOX](https://www.foxsports.com/stories/soccer/tyler-adams-says-hostile-home-crowds-good-world-cup-experience-usmnt)). Expect SoFi crowds that are pro-USA but corporate-quiet and 25–40% neutral/opposition; **Seattle June 19 vs Australia is likely the loudest genuinely pro-US room of the group stage** (Sounders season-ticket culture, modest Australian base). Vs Türkiye at SoFi June 25, the Turkish diaspora is in the wrong state (§3) — USA keeps crowd control. Net: the market's host bump for the US is fair on venue/travel and slightly rich on atmosphere.

**Canada.** BMO Field (45,736 — the smallest venue) for the June 12 opener vs Bosnia is a real home crowd in a tight, loud park; Vancouver (June 18 vs Qatar, June 24 vs Switzerland) similar. Canada gets the full channel at human scale — but over only ~46–54k seats, the absolute referee-pressure dose is the lowest of the three hosts.

## 5. June 2026 wildcards: the visa wall, ICE, Iran, and a strike vote

This is where 2026 diverges from every prior World Cup — *who is allowed to show up* is now a live variable ([CFR: "The U.S. is co-hosting the World Cup, but much of the world can't attend"](https://www.cfr.org/articles/the-u-s-is-co-hosting-the-world-cup-but-much-of-the-world-cant-attend)):

- **Travel bans:** **Haiti and Iran face full US entry bans; Senegal and Côte d'Ivoire partial restrictions; DR Congo is blocked via Ebola-outbreak rules**. Fans from these countries attend US games only if they are US residents/dual nationals ([Front Office Sports](https://frontofficesports.com/travel-visa-issues-hang-over-world-cup/)). **Egypt, Ghana, Jordan, Morocco, Uruguay and Uzbekistan sit on the administration's visa-pause list**; Moroccan supporter groups report dozens of unexplained denials, Ghanaian applicants a ~98% rejection rate ([Modern Ghana](https://www.modernghana.com/news/1492848/denied-before-kick-off-how-americas-visa-wall.html), [TRT](https://www.trtworld.com/article/a1e13c9678bf)).
- **Interview backlogs:** Colombia's B-visa queue ran ~**696 days** and the Bogotá embassy then *suspended* processing after a diplomatic spat ([Boundless](https://www.boundless.com/blog/can-you-get-a-us-tourist-visa-in-time-for-the-2026-world-cup), [Travel Weekly](https://www.travelweekly.com/Travel-News/Government/Urgency-over-visa-backlog-ahead-of-Olympics-and-World-Cup)). Net effect across nationalities: away support = resident diaspora, with traveling fans largely filtered to visa-waiver countries (UK, EU, Japan, Korea, Australia...) ([Travel and Tour World](https://www.travelandtourworld.com/news/article/d4u4qk360m66/)).
- **Iran — the extreme case (breaking, June 9):** the US is at war footing with Tehran; the squad's player visas cleared only on June 5–6 while **15+ federation staff were denied**; the team is based in **Tijuana**, not Arizona ([Al Jazeera June 7](https://www.aljazeera.com/sports/2026/6/7/irans-world-cup-squad-lands-in-mexico-amid-us-visa-row), [ESPN](https://www.espn.com/soccer/story/_/id/48984862/iran-usa-world-cup-visas-officials-2026)). Today, **FIFA revoked Iran's entire 8% fan-ticket allocation for all three group games** ([ESPN](https://www.espn.com/soccer/story/_/id/49008116/world-cup-tickets-iran-fans-revoked-says-country-federation), [Al Jazeera June 9](https://www.aljazeera.com/sports/2026/6/9/iran-says-us-have-revoked-world-cup-ticket-allocation-for-their-supporters)). With Iran residents banned anyway, Team Melli's support at SoFi is purely **Tehrangeles** — the world's largest Iranian community outside Iran (§3) — a diaspora that is heavily exile/anti-regime in politics yet historically turns out for the team; LA officials have announced **extra security for both SoFi Iran matches and confirmed no ICE deployment at LA World Cup events** ([Al Jazeera June 2](https://www.aljazeera.com/sports/2026/6/2/world-cup-no-ice-deployment-extra-security-for-iran-games-in-los-angeles), [Fox News](https://www.foxnews.com/sports/officials-lay-ices-role-los-angeles-world-cup-plans-announce-boosted-security-iran-games)).
- **ICE and Latino attendance:** 120+ civil-rights organizations including the ACLU issued a formal *travel advisory* about attending ([Al Jazeera May 26](https://www.aljazeera.com/sports/2026/5/26/minorities-fear-world-cup-threat-of-ice-and-how-immigration-is-enforced), [Amnesty](https://www.amnesty.org/en/latest/news/2026/03/global-fifa-and-world-cup-hosts-must-prevent-tournament-becoming-a-threat-to-fans-and-communities/)); some LA fans are openly boycotting ([LA Public Press](https://lapublicpress.org/2026/05/ice-world-cup-soccer-boycott-la/)); VP Vance's task-force "joke" — visitors "will have to go home, otherwise they'll have to talk to Secretary Noem" — went viral as a threat ([Newsweek](https://www.newsweek.com/jd-vance-jokes-detaining-world-cup-2026-visitors-fifa-2068927)). Federal officials have since told LA County **civil immigration enforcement will not take place at World Cup games or fan fests**. Betting-relevant residual: a marginal damper on undocumented-adjacent walk-up attendance in LA/Houston — but Mexico's matches are sold out regardless; the chilling effect shows up in *neutral-fixture* demand, not El Tri demand.
- **Labor:** SoFi's ~2,000 food/beverage workers voted **96% to authorize a strike** on June 6 (ICE's role in security + pay + FIFA suite subcontracting), with talks ongoing June 8 and the operator claiming contingency staffing ([NPR](https://www.npr.org/2026/06/06/nx-s1-5848682/sofi-stadium-strike-world-cup), [LAMag](https://lamag.com/sports/sofi-stadium-workers-authorize-strike-ahead-of-world-cup-opener/)). Worst case is operational chaos around the June 12 US opener and the Iran fixtures — an atmosphere/logistics tail risk at the single most loaded venue.
- **Macro:** US inbound arrivals fell **14.1% YoY in April 2026**, and WTTC projects a **$12.5B** international-spend loss for the year ([Travel and Tour World](https://www.travelandtourworld.com/news/article/united-states-tourism-in-peril-as-international-visitors-evade-america-as-2026-travel-figures-collapse/)) — fewer traveling neutrals means diaspora weight in crowds is *higher* than any pre-2025 model assumes. The 1994 benchmark (record 3.59M total, 68,991/game, ~96% capacity, famously immigrant-driven — [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1994_FIFA_World_Cup), [Guinness](https://www.guinnessworldrecords.com/news/2014/6/world-cup-rewind-america-goes-crazy-for-soccer-in-1994-57852)) still points the same direction: US World Cup crowds are diaspora + curious neutrals who adopt underdogs and Latin teams, now with a corporate crust on top.

## 6. Atmosphere quality: where crowds will be loud, flat, or fake

- **Dynamic pricing has bifurcated the tournament:** sold-out diaspora fixtures (all Mexico, Brazil-east-coast, Argentina-central, Colombia–Portugal) vs discounted neutral fixtures (Czechia–South Africa, Atlanta, noon June 18; Austria–Jordan, Levi's, June 17 midnight ET; Bosnia–Qatar, Seattle, June 24) that risk visible empty seats at 65–75k venues even when "distributed" ([ESPN sticker shock](https://www.espn.com/soccer/story/_/id/48947095/2026-fifa-world-cup-sticker-shock-ugly-cost-beautiful-game-grand-event)). For props: low-energy neutral fixtures mute the referee channel entirely — price those off team priors only.
- **The final = corporate.** $7.3k get-ins, ~35% hospitality holds, On Location packages to $73k (§2). Expect a Super Bowl-style crowd: late to seats, quiet, no referee pressure either way. The *loudest* knockout rooms will be Azteca R32 (Mexico), Miami R32 July 3 (likely Argentina, 6pm heat), and Dallas/Atlanta semis if Latin teams reach them.
- **Azteca remains the intimidation outlier** — altitude (≈20% less air density), enclosed acoustics, and a 60-year mythology of visitors wilting ([stadium guides](https://stadiumsoflegends.com/estadio-azteca/), [CF Montréal](https://en.cfmontreal.com/news/estadio-azteca-one-most-historic-soccer-stadiums-world)). It hosts five matches including Colombia–Uzbekistan — Colombia inherits the cauldron-and-altitude package twice without leaving CONMEBOL-style conditions.
- **US neutrals adopt underdogs and Latin teams** — Copa 2024's gate hierarchy (Argentina/Colombia/Mexico/Brazil >> USA) and the Colombian gate-crash at the Miami final ([2024 Copa final](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_Copa_Am%C3%A9rica_final)) are the live rehearsal of exactly the 2026 east-coast/Miami crowd dynamics predicted here.

## Key takeaways for the betting playbook

1. **Crowd composition is predictable from census + visa policy + resale data, and the market only prices it for the three hosts.** Diaspora-backed "away" teams (Ecuador, Colombia, Senegal, Ghana, Croatia-in-Toronto) carry unpriced referee-channel value worth ~0.05–0.10 goals / 1–3pp win probability in even matches.
2. **The channel is real and crowd-mediated:** 63% of PL pens to home sides, 15.5% fewer fouls against home teams under crowd noise ([Nevill et al.](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/32117163_Influence_of_crowd_noise_on_soccer_refereeing_consistency_in_soccer)), yellow-card bias ([Unkelbach & Memmert](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20733209/)), 2x added time favoring the crowd team in close games ([Garicano et al.](https://direct.mit.edu/rest/article-abstract/87/2/208/57539/Favoritism-Under-Social-Pressure)), and COVID ghost games as the controlled experiment ([Nature](https://www.nature.com/articles/s41598-021-00784-8)).
3. **Express it in props first, 1X2 last:** penalty-awarded-to-X, opponent yellow-card overs, and added-time/late-goal markets monetize the channel directly; 1X2/AH shading only when stacked with altitude, heat or travel edges per the ch. 7 stacking rule.
4. **Ecuador vs Germany (MetLife, June 25) is the single most mispriced crowd in the group stage** — 657k NY-metro Ecuadorians vs a corporate/tourist German contingent, in a match the odds will price as heavily pro-Germany.
5. **Colombia is the stealth "fourth host":** two group games in Mexico (no US visa wall, Latin crowd adoption, altitude fit) then Miami — the hottest non-final resale ticket — with a top-7 global ticket-buying base that lives in Florida/Queens. Their group-stage environment edge rivals the actual hosts'.
6. **Mexico's channel extends through the knockout tree:** ~95% Azteca crowds, then either Azteca R32 or a SoFi R32 in the largest Mexican-origin county in America (37–40M nationally). The 2011 Rose Bowl final (93,420, pro-Mexico) is the template; "reach QF" support is structural, not sentimental.
7. **The visa wall converts away support into a pure diaspora variable:** full bans (Haiti, Iran), partial (Senegal, Côte d'Ivoire), Ebola block (DR Congo), visa pauses (Egypt, Ghana, Jordan, Morocco, Uruguay, Uzbekistan), 696-day Colombian queues — traveling fans now skew to visa-waiver Europe/Asia; everyone else's crowd is whoever already lives here.
8. **Iran (June 9, breaking): FIFA revoked the entire Iranian fan allocation** for all three games; with residents banned, Team Melli's support = Tehrangeles. Treat both SoFi Iran fixtures as high-variance, high-security, politically charged crowds — bet cards/volatility, not direction.
9. **USA home advantage is overrated on atmosphere:** the opener was unsold on June 8 at hostile dynamic prices, Copa 2024 showed USMNT outnumbered "in games that mattered" (Dempsey), and SoFi adds a 96% strike-authorization vote. Seattle June 19 is their one true cauldron; don't pay an atmosphere premium at SoFi.
10. **Fade the channel entirely in dead-crowd fixtures** (Egypt–Iran Seattle, Czechia–South Africa Atlanta noon, Austria–Jordan and Jordan–Algeria late-night Levi's, Bosnia–Qatar Seattle): empty premium seats, no referee pressure — model on team priors and consider card-unders.
11. **Knockout crowds get *less* partisan as stakes rise** — ~35% hospitality holds, $7.3k final get-ins — so crowd-channel edges peak in the group stage and at Azteca/Miami R32s, then decay toward a corporate final. Weight the channel accordingly in stage pricing.
12. **Watch June 11–13 as calibration:** Azteca decibels, Haiti–Scotland's diaspora-vs-Tartan split, Brazil–Morocco's Ironbound turnout, and whether SoFi's strike/ICE overhang visibly thins the US opener. Update fixture-level crowd priors before MD2 pricing.
13. **Mind the schedule discrepancy fixed here:** Mexico MD2/MD3 are June 18 (Guadalajara) and June 24 (Azteca) — chapter 5's June 19/25 dates are off by one; all watchlist dates above were verified against group-guide schedules this week.
14. **Macro tailwind for the thesis:** April 2026 US inbound arrivals −14.1% YoY and a projected $12.5B visitor-spend shortfall mean fewer traveling neutrals than any prior model assumed — diaspora share of every crowd rises, amplifying every asymmetry in this chapter.
